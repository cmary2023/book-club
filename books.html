Our Featured Books

Kill Creatures

By Rory Power

"Kill Creatures" is a young adult thriller by Rory Power, revolving around Nan, who is haunted by the disappearance of her three best friends in Saltcedar Canyon a year prior. A memorial vigil is interrupted by the shocking return of one of the girls, Luce, who was presumed dead, and Nan knows the truth: she killed them. The novel explores themes of friendship, betrayal, jealousy, and revenge, with a twist ending.

  • Dark thriller: Opens with Nan confessing she killed her three best friends.
  • Thrilling plot: The story is full of twists and turns.
  • Well-developed characters: The characters are well-developed and relatable.

Never Flinch

By Stephen King

The story centered on two interwoven storylines: a serial killer targeting innocent people in Buckeye City and a feminist activist facing threats and disruption on her lecture tour.

  • Holly Gibney returns to solve two dark cases.
  • Released May 2025, King’s 6th book with Holly.
  • Explores trauma, justice, and obsession.

We can never leave

By H.E. Edgmon

The story follows 5 teenage travelers who wake to find their community has disappeared overnight. They will have to work together to figure out what happened as each of them are trying to deal with their own secrets.

  • YA fantasy mystery about missing travelers.
  • Published June 2025 by Wednesday Books.
  • Five flawed teens face secrets and trauma.

One Dark Kiss

By Rebecca Zanetti

It's a dark romantasy with a modern Snow White twist featuring Rosalie, a clever lawyer, and Alexei, a dangerous heir to a powerful Russian mob and social media empire.

  • The story is set in a dark fantasy world.
  • Dark romantasy twist on Snow White.
  • Opposites-attract duo story line.

The Poppy Fields

By Nikki Erlick

A novel by Nikki Erlick about a fictional treatment center in the California desert where people can sleep through the pain of loss.

  • Experimental therapy lets people “sleep off” grief.
  • Four strangers take a life‑changing road trip.
  • Explores memory, love, and identity.
